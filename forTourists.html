Tourist Information

Although Taniti is a small island, its infrastructure is well-developed to cater to tourists. The island has a range of accommodations, from luxury resorts to budget-friendly hotels. Visitors can enjoy a variety of local and international cuisines at the island's restaurants and cafes. Transportation on the island is convenient, with options for car rentals, taxis, and public buses. Additionally, there are several tour operators that offer guided tours and activities to help visitors explore the island's natural beauty and cultural heritage.

Taniti has two supermarkets, a hospital, 10 restaurants, a convenience store open 24 hours a day.

Frequently Requested Information

  • Power outlets are 120 volts (same as the US).
  • Alcohol is not allowed to be served or sold between midnight and 9:00 AM.
  • The drinking age on Taniti is 18 years old.
  • Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English. Very little English is spoken in rural areas and by the older population.
  • There is one hospital and several clinics. The hospital has many multilingual employees.
  • Violent crime is very rare on Taniti, however as tourism has increased there have been more reports of pick pocketings and petty crime.
  • Taniti enjoys a large number of national holidays and many tourist attractions and restaurants will be closed on holidays, please plan accordingly.
  • Taniti uses the US dollar as its currency, but many businesses will also accept Euros and Yen. Several banks can facilitate currency exchange and many businesses accept most major credit cards.
